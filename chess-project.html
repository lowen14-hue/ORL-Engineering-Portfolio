UConn Themed Chess Board Assembly

A comprehensive chess set design project created in Siemens NX, combining advanced surface modeling, parametric CAD techniques, and presentation-quality rendering for a final engineering design project at the University of Connecticut.

Project Overview

This project represents a comprehensive approach to mechanical design and CAD modeling. The objective was to design and model a complete chess set with a UConn theme, applying advanced engineering principles and modern CAD workflows. Every piece was carefully modeled to ensure geometric accuracy, manufacturability, and aesthetic appeal.

Design Process

The design process began with concept sketches and geometric analysis of traditional chess piece proportions. Using Siemens NX, I developed parametric models that allowed for rapid iteration and refinement. The knight piece became the focal point, incorporating UConn Husky branding through sculpted surface details and stylized geometry that maintains the piece's functional chess identity.

Each piece was modeled with manufacturing considerations, ensuring that the designs could be produced through additive manufacturing processes while maintaining structural integrity and aesthetic quality. The assembly was constrained and configured to demonstrate proper chess piece placement and board interaction.

Outcomes & Learnings

This project reinforced the importance of balancing aesthetic design with engineering constraints. Working within NX's parametric environment allowed me to explore design variations efficiently and understand how CAD decisions impact manufacturing feasibility. The project demonstrated the complete workflow from concept to presentation-ready rendering, preparing me for professional engineering design roles.

The experience of blending themed branding with functional engineering taught me that great design happens at the intersection of art and science—where creative vision meets technical rigor and manufacturing reality.
