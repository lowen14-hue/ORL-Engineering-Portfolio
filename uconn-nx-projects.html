UConn NX Projects

Coordinate-Driven Curve Generation & Geometry Analysis


This project focused on creating and evaluating coordinate-defined spline curves in Siemens NX. Using a set of spatial coordinates, spline geometry was generated and refined through geometric constraints to produce smooth, accurate curves. Curvature analysis tools were then used to evaluate the geometry and verify the continuity of the resulting spline. The project emphasized the relationship between coordinate data, spline creation, and geometric analysis, demonstrating how precise curve definition supports accurate CAD modeling and engineering design.

Aircraft Surface Modeling & Section Analysis

This project focused on developing a detailed aircraft model in Siemens NX using surface modeling techniques and geometric analysis. Cross-sectional views were used throughout the design process to evaluate the continuity and accuracy of the aircraft's profile, allowing for refinement of the fuselage, wings, tail, and nose geometry.


The completed model demonstrates the use of curve networks, surface creation, and section analysis to construct a smooth, aerodynamically shaped aircraft. Rendering tools were then used to apply materials and finishes, creating a realistic visualization of the final design.
